title: SIPP dilemma
date: 2026-05-08
---

From April 2027, unused SIPP funds form part of your estate for IHT — including every pound contributed under the old rules, with no grandfathering.

The government calls this a prospective change. But there's an uncomfortable either/or. If the change is legitimate, then for decades SIPPs were marketed without material disclosure of the risk that the IHT-free inheritance leg could be unilaterally removed for money already committed. That's a mis-selling argument. Conversely, if the change isn't legitimate — because it rewrites the economics of irreversible past decisions — then the legislation itself is unfair.

"Because it's the government" isn't a satisfying resolution. It's just an answer about who has the power to avoid the question.
