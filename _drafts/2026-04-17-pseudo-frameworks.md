Before agentic workflows, we might argue that frameworks were created despite high costs because the process of an outsider disentangling a subsystem from it's exemplar whole was perceived to be frustrating and difficult for humans. Now with agents, we have no concept of an agent being frustrated and we sit somewhere on the curve of agents being much better at this or humans largely discounting the frustration of humans working with agents on this disentangling and re-implementation problem.
