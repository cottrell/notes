The SIPP operator is FCA-regulated. The tax treatment is not.

That is the gap. The provider is bound by fairness rules; Parliament is not. So the saver commits under terms that can be rewritten later without explicit disclosure.

There are only two clean versions of the bargain: fixed tax treatment, or explicit sovereign risk. The current setup offers neither.
